Occupational History & Professional Experience

Dr. Ugochukwu Albert Elenwa’s professional journey is defined by a lifetime of commitment to education, administration, and leadership. Over the years, he has excelled across teaching, academic supervision, and logistics management, building a career grounded in discipline, integrity, and service. His work reflects the fusion of academic expertise with practical leadership in both educational and corporate environments.

Lecturer II – Ignatius Ajuru University of Education

2025 – Present

Currently serving as Lecturer II, responsible for teaching, academic coordination, student assessment, and supervision of research projects. He also contributes to policy formulation and curriculum improvement within the Department of Educational Management.

Lecturer – Covenant College of Education, Aba

2023 – 2024

Engaged in teaching and research at the institution affiliated with Abia State University. Focused on curriculum development, student evaluation, and research publication on educational planning and leadership.

Classroom Teacher – Rivers State SUBEB

2014 – 2019

Taught social science subjects, mentored students, coordinated school programs, and contributed to school administration. Promoted effective learning strategies and positive student behavior.

Logistics Officer – Ponticelli Nigeria Ltd. (Total E&P Project)

2009 – 2012

Managed logistics operations and transport coordination for large-scale industrial projects. Oversaw equipment mobilization, workforce movement, and resource allocation to support site operations.

Community Liaison Officer – Homan Engineering Nigeria Ltd.

2007 – 2009

Served as a liaison between company and host communities to ensure smooth project operations. Mediated community issues, handled personnel administration, and promoted conflict-free collaboration.

Classroom Teacher – Community Secondary School, Osiakpu

1999 – 2004

Developed lesson plans, taught social science subjects, and coordinated student welfare and extracurricular activities. Built foundational teaching experience that shaped his career in education.

Assistant Field Supervisor – Elf Agricultural Project

1996 – 1997

Supervised farm operations, kept production records, and supported crop and livestock management programs. Gained early experience in coordination and agricultural education.

Commitment to Service and Growth

Throughout his career, Dr. Elenwa has demonstrated a steadfast commitment to professionalism, knowledge sharing, and institutional advancement. His diverse experiences across education, administration, and logistics have made him a versatile leader with a deep understanding of people, systems, and performance. He continues to inspire excellence and strives to build institutions that reflect the highest standards of education and service.
